# Binghamton Rumble Ponies: Attendance Decline Analysis

**Prepared:** April 2026 | **Data:** 2023-2025 MiLB seasons | **Level:** Double-A Eastern League

---

## Executive Summary

The Binghamton Rumble Ponies have experienced a **37.9% cumulative attendance decline** over three seasons, falling from 26th to **dead last (30th of 30)** among all Double-A teams. The decline is not explained by on-field performance (home win% actually improved to 70.5% in 2025) or promotional effort (Binghamton runs *more* promotions per game than the league's top teams). The data points to **structural market challenges** compounded by an **ownership transition** in mid-2024. This document identifies what the data shows, what successful peer teams do differently, and where actionable opportunities exist.

---

## 1. The Decline by the Numbers

| Season | Home Games | Total Attendance | Avg/Game | Fill Rate | AA Rank |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|----------|-----------|---------|
| 2023   | 65        | 206,911         | 3,183    | 52.9%     | 26/30   |
| 2024   | 63        | 146,061         | 2,318    | 38.6%     | 29/30   |
| 2025   | 61        | 120,627         | 1,977    | 32.9%     | 30/30   |

- **2023 to 2024:** -27.2% (worst single-year drop of ANY Double-A team)
- **2024 to 2025:** -14.7% (2nd worst, behind Arkansas at -22.8%)
- **Lost 86,284 total fans** across two seasons
- The stadium (Mirabito, capacity 6,012) is now **two-thirds empty** on an average night

### Context: The Paradox of Winning

| Season | Home Win% | Avg Attendance |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| 2023   | 60.0%     | 3,183         |
| 2024   | 42.9%     | 2,318         |
| 2025   | **70.5%** | **1,977**     |

**Key finding:** Binghamton's best on-field season (2025) produced their worst attendance. Winning is not driving fans to the stadium. The problem is elsewhere.

## 2. Where the Bleeding Happens

### Early Season Collapse (April)

| Month | 2023  | 2024  | 2025    | AA Avg (2025) |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|---------------|
| April | 2,189 | 1,561 | **971** | 3,748         |
| May   | 2,752 | 2,228 | 1,551   | 4,451         |
| June  | 3,864 | 2,706 | 2,339   | 4,257         |
| July  | 3,509 | 2,932 | 2,210   | 4,335         |
| Aug   | 3,275 | 2,148 | 2,416   | 4,133         |
| Sept  | 3,708 | 2,371 | 1,954   | 4,357         |

April 2025: **971 avg fans** (16% fill). Cold weather in upstate NY makes early-season games nearly unviable. But even warm-weather months (June-August) are 45-55% below league averages.

### Day-of-Week Attendance (2025)

| Day       | Games | Avg   | vs. AA Avg |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----------|
| Friday    | 11    | 2,605 | ~60% of AA Friday avg |
| Saturday  | 11    | 2,117 | below most teams' Tuesday avg |
| Tuesday   | 9     | 1,430 | catastrophically low |

**Their Saturday (2,117) draws less than 22 of 30 AA teams draw on a random weekday.**

### Weather Doesn't Explain It All

| Temp Range | 2023  | 2025  |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Cold (<55F)   | 993 (2 games) | 1,382 (7 games) |
| Mild (55-70F) | 2,911 | 1,811 |
| Warm (71-85F) | 3,257 | 2,141 |
| Hot (>85F)    | 4,082 | 2,168 |

More cold games in 2025 hurt, but even warm-weather games dropped 34% from 2023. The decline is across ALL conditions.

## 3. The Promotion Paradox

### Binghamton Runs MORE Promos Than Top Teams

| Metric | Top 5 AA Teams | Bottom 5 AA Teams | Binghamton |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Avg Attendance | 6,026 | 2,725 | **1,977** |
| Promos Per Game | 1.5 | 1.8 | **2.3** |
| % Games w/ Promos | 93.9% | 91.5% | 88.5% |
| Fireworks Lift | +24% | +68% | +50% |
| Theme Night Lift | +5% | +16% | +19% |

**Promotions work in relative terms** -- fireworks add ~900 fans, theme nights add ~350. But the baseline they lift FROM is so low that the result is still far below league norms. Throwing more promotions at this problem will not fix it.

### Top 5 Games Tell the Story (2025)

| Att.  | Date   | What Happened |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| 5,223 | Jun 4  | "Baseball in Education Day" -- school group event |
| 4,507 | Jul 4  | Independence Day Fireworks |
| 3,865 | Aug 15 | Fireworks + Giveaway + Theme Night (triple-stack) |
| 3,758 | May 21 | Wednesday day game -- likely school groups |
| 3,398 | Aug 31 | Bark in the Park + Bluey + Kids' Club |

**The only games that approach normal attendance are group sales events and July 4th.** Education days are the single most effective lever this team has.

## 4. What Successful Teams Do Differently

### Richmond Flying Squirrels (6,768 avg -- #1 in AA)

- **Club Membership Model:** Wine Wednesdays, Funnville Friday Happy Hours, Fireworks Saturdays -- recurring weekly rituals, not one-off events
- **Fewer but bigger promos:** 1.5 promos/game vs. Binghamton's 2.3
- Heritage nights average **7,866** fans; entertainment nights average **8,398**
- Rally towel giveaway to "first 60,000 fans" over opening week (creates urgency + multi-game incentive)
- **Market:** Richmond MSA ~1.3M, growing economy, state capital

### Frisco RoughRiders (5,724 avg -- Top 5)

- **Theme night dominant:** 50 theme nights per season -- nearly every game has a unique identity
- Food deals as constant value-add (27 food deal nights)
- **Market:** DFW metro, massive population base

### Somerset Patriots (5,449 avg -- Top 5)

- Theme-night focused (42 per season) with fewer giveaways
- Minimal food deals (only 3) -- they don't compete on price
- **Market:** NJ suburban, NYC metro spillover

### Portland Sea Dogs (5,817 avg -- Top 5)

- **Community-first approach:** 25 community nights (highest in AA)
- Community events average 6,135 fans
- **Market:** Portland, ME -- small market like Binghamton but with tourism economy

## 5. The Market Reality

### Binghamton by the Numbers

- **City population:** ~46,000 (peaked at 85,000 in 1950s)
- **MSA population:** 247,000 (declining at -0.54%/year)
- **Median household income:** $45,578
- **Poverty rate:** 32.6%
- **Manufacturing base:** Lost 70% since 1990 -- one of the largest declines of any US metro
- **Stadium:** Mirabito Stadium, built 1992 (34 years old), capacity 6,012
- **Ownership:** Sold to Diamond Baseball Holdings (DBH) in April 2024; DBH now owns 33 MiLB teams

### How This Compares to Successful Peers

| Factor | Binghamton | Richmond | Portland |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| MSA Population | 247K (declining) | 1.3M (growing) | 550K (growing) |
| Stadium Age | 1992 (34 yrs) | 2010 (16 yrs) | 1994 (32 yrs) |
| Economy | Shrinking manufacturing | State capital, diverse | Tourism + healthcare |
| Avg Attendance | 1,977 | 6,768 | 5,817 |

Portland is the most instructive comparison. Similar stadium age, small-market, cold-weather, yet draws nearly 3x the fans. Portland benefits from a stronger local economy and a tourism-friendly downtown location.

## 6. Ownership Transition Impact

Diamond Baseball Holdings acquired the Rumble Ponies in **April 2024** -- right as the sharpest decline began.

- DBH owns 33 teams (25%+ of affiliated MiLB)
- The press release stated "all front office staff remain in place"
- Ownership transitions often create uncertainty that affects season ticket renewals, sponsorship, and community partnerships
- **However**, DBH has resources and a multi-team playbook. They know what works at their other properties

**Research question:** What are DBH's best-performing teams doing, and can those playbooks be applied to Binghamton?

## 7. Actionable Recommendations for Further Research

### High-Confidence Opportunities (data-supported)

1. **Double Down on Group Sales / Education Days**
   - The highest-attended game (5,223) was a school day. Two of the top 5 games were likely school groups
   - Binghamton has Binghamton University (17,000+ students) and several school districts within the MSA
   - Research: What % of current attendance comes from group sales? What is the ceiling?

2. **Shift from Promo Volume to Promo Quality**
   - At 2.3 promos/game, Binghamton is already saturating. Top teams run fewer (1.5/game) but more impactful events
   - Cut low-impact promos (food deals average only 1,575 fans) and invest in fewer, bigger signature events
   - Research: Which specific promotions have the highest marginal lift after controlling for day-of-week and weather?

3. **Build Recurring Weekly Rituals (Richmond Model)**
   - Richmond's Wine Wednesdays, Happy Hour Fridays, and Fireworks Saturdays create habits, not one-off decisions
   - Every game night has an identity. Fans know "Friday = happy hour + fireworks" without looking at the schedule
   - Research: Can Binghamton implement a weekly rhythm (e.g., "Taco Tuesday", "Thirsty Thursday", "Fireworks Friday")?

4. **Rethink April**
   - 971 avg fans in April 2025. Cold weather + school in session = near-empty stadium
   - Options: push for more school-day games in April, partner with youth sports leagues, or accept lower April capacity and focus marketing spend on May-August
   - Research: What do other cold-weather AA teams (Portland, Erie, New Hampshire) do in April?

### Medium-Confidence Opportunities (needs more data)

5. **Pricing Strategy Audit**
   - With 32.6% poverty rate and $45K median income, pricing sensitivity is high
   - Research: What are ticket/concession prices vs. peer teams? Is there a value perception problem?

6. **Diamond Baseball Holdings Playbook**
   - DBH owns 33 teams. Some are performing well. What do their successful properties do?
   - Research: Compare attendance trends at other DBH-owned teams pre/post acquisition

7. **Community Partnership / Anchor Tenant Strategy**
   - Portland's community-first approach (25 community nights, 6,135 avg) works in a similar market
   - Binghamton University partnership? Hospital/healthcare system (largest employer sector)?
   - Research: Map top 10 employers in Broome County -> group sales pipeline

8. **Digital/Social Engagement Audit**
   - MiLB industry research shows data-driven segmentation and targeted messaging drive the biggest gains
   - Research: What is Binghamton's social media following/engagement vs. peers? Email list size? CRM usage?

### Longer-Term Strategic Questions

9. **Facility Investment**
   - Mirabito Stadium (1992) has had some upgrades (new scoreboards in 2024) but is aging
   - The biggest MiLB attendance gains in 2025 came from teams that moved to new facilities
   - Research: What is the ROI case for a significant facility investment? Is there municipal appetite?

10. **Market Viability Assessment**
    - Binghamton's population has declined 46% from its peak. The MSA is shrinking. Manufacturing is gone.
    - At what point does the market become unviable for affiliated baseball?
    - Research: Are there other MiLB teams in similarly-sized declining markets that have succeeded? (Possible comp: Akron, +8.4% growth in 2025, in a similarly post-industrial market)
